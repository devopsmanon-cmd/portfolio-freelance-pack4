Comparison: DevOps approach vs local agencies

This page explains differences in method, structure and transparency so you can choose based on facts, not vague marketing.

Quick read

Manon approach (Web + DevOps)

  • SEO structure + conversion + technical execution in one line
  • Direct communication, no multiple layers
  • Clear pricing and explicit options
  • Long-term view: performance, stability, scalability

Typical local agency model

  • Strong design possible, SEO depth varies
  • Multiple contacts (sales + PM + production)
  • Pricing can be less transparent
  • Advanced DevOps often optional or outsourced

Detailed comparison

1) Method

Manon: short scoping, fast execution, milestone validation.

Agency: longer cycles and internal coordination.

2) Price transparency

Manon: visible packages, separated options, explicit payment.

Agency: custom quotes with possible extra layers.

3) SEO structure

Manon: clear architecture, service/zone pages, strategic linking.

Agency: SEO quality depends on internal specialization.

4) DevOps / performance

Manon: clean technical base from the start.

Agency: sometimes added later or outsourced.

5) Timelines

Manon: package-based timelines with clear scope.

Agency: often longer if internal pipeline is full.

6) Client relationship

Manon: direct and agile collaboration.

Agency: more formal process, sometimes less flexible.
